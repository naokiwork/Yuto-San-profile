A. Nonlinear Systems via Takagi–Sugeno (T–S) Fuzzy Modeling

Many real-world systems are nonlinear and cannot be expressed by a single simple equation.

Dr. Asai utilizes the Takagi–Sugeno (T–S) fuzzy model to approximate nonlinear systems as a weighted combination of linear subsystems. This approach allows:
	•	Analytical tractability
	•	Stability analysis via Lyapunov methods
	•	Systematic controller synthesis

This modeling framework serves as the foundation of nearly all his research contributions.

⸻

B. Output Feedback Control (When Full State Measurement Is Impossible)

In many engineering systems:
	•	Internal states are partially unknown
	•	Sensors cannot measure all variables
	•	Premise variables may not be directly available

Dr. Asai focuses on output feedback control, where the controller uses only measurable outputs rather than full state vectors.

Key aspects include:
	•	Static output feedback stabilization
	•	Observer-based control design
	•	Stability guarantees without requiring derivative information

This line of research enhances practical applicability in real systems.
